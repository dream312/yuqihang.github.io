The Mediation of Sensory Prediction through Dual-stream Cortical Feedbacks

Project Description

Predictions are constantly generated from diverse sources to optimize cognitive functions in the ever-changing environment. However, the neural origin and generation process of top-down induced prediction remain elusive. We hypothesized that motor-based and memory-based predictions are mediated by distinct feedback networks from motor and memory systems to the sensory cortices.

My Contribution

1. Read review articles to understand one machine learning technique in cognitive neuroscience: multi-voxel pattern analysis(MVPA) and multivariate cross-classification(MVCC)
2. Investigated the general structure, analysis stream, and a typical searchlight decoding of The Decoding Toolbox (TDT) for MVPA
3. Wrote MATLAB codes to perform a leave-one-run-out cross-validated searchlight MVPA, including: extraction of parameter estimates (“betas”) from a sphere of voxels with a certain center; partitioning of the data for training and test; classification using support vector machine (SVM); accuracy calculation of classifying test data in sphere.

Comparable Semantic Representation in Neural and Computer Systems

Project Description

Semantic representation has been studied independently in neuroscience and computer science. A deep understanding of human neural computations and the revolution to strong artificial intelligence appeal for a joint force in the language domain. We investigated comparable representational formats of lexical semantics between these two complex systems with fine temporal resolution neural recordings.

My Contribution

1. Recruited participants and trained junior students on study procedures
2. Preprocessed raw EEG data: artifact rejection and correction; averaging; filtering
3. Analyzed preprocessed data to detect amplitude and latency of ERP waves, GFP, and topography
4. Created word stimulus set by filtering out true words from English Lexicon Project and selecting non-words from Wuggy database
5. Revised Python codes to present new experimental paradigms and to include triggers(event codes)
6. Redesigned Python programs to include event codes (triggers)
7. Wrote Python programs to analyze cosine similarity between two words using Glove models

Psycholinguistics, EEG data collection and analysis, Natural Language Processing, Python

Psychological Processing of the Feeling of being Moved from Art

Project Description

It has recently been claimed by neuroscientists that intense aesthetic experiences involve introspection. Since this claim is based on only indirect evidence, here we provide a direct test of the aesthetic experience-introspection hypothesis. We investigated how the feeling of being moved from art is influenced by or correlated with the mental processes of introspection.

My Contribution

1. Wrote research proposal to clarify research background, research purpose, construct experimental design and establish research hypothesis
2. Adjusted requirements for the type of paintings selected for research by doing pilot study
3. Selected paintings as the psychological stimuli that fulfill the research requirement which spans in three dimensions: moving/not moving, positive/negative, landscape/people
4. Conducted human experimental design using Qualtrics
5. Designed research poster to recruit participants from home and aboard
6. Finished the collection of behavioral data of 50 participants independently
7. Conducted qualitative coding to rate the degree of self-reflection/ introspective behavior of subjects
8. Conducted data wrangling using Excel and SPSS
9. Used SPSS to carry multinominal logistic regression, multiple linear regression, multi-level logistic modeling for preliminary data analysis
10. Used SPSS to carry chi-square test of independence to test the association between the feeling of being moved and type of introspection and accomplished preliminary data visualization

My Skills

Empirical Aesthetics, Qualtrics, Qualitative Research Methods, SPSS
